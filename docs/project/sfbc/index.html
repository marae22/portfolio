San Francisco Bicycle Coalition
February 2017

OVERVIEW

The SFBC is a non-profit organization that is committed to making the city of San Francisco safer for all cyclists.

PROBLEM

As a concept project SFBC needed a strategy and design for digital tools that would engage their members more frequently and give members a sense of active contribution to the SFBC’s larger goals.

SOLUTION

A mobile app that allows San Francisco Bicycle Coalition (SFBC) members to easily report and track issues

TEAM & DURATION

My role was co-UX Designer with an additional focus on content strategy and visual design. The duration of this project was 2 weeks.

TOOLS & METHODS

  • Contextual Inquiry
  • User Surveys & Interviews
  • Sketching
  • Prototyping
  • Usability Testing

FINAL PRODUCT

RESEARCH & DESIGN

SURVEYS

After doing some surveying we found out that most cyclists have heard about SFBC, but over 75% of cyclists said they were not members.

INTERVIEWS

We interviewed some frequent cyclists and came away with similar trends regarding personal safety, road conditions, and lack of safe parking.

WHAT WE GATHERED

Based on the synthesis of our surveys, interviews and contextual inquiries, we came up with two key insights.

  1. There is no official way to report problems to anyone.
  2. There is no visibility of actions taken.

WHAT'S IN THE APP

Based on our key insights, we created a list of features to include in our app.

  1. Report an issue
  2. Status of reported issue / up voting
  3. Routes / multiple route options
  4. Parking options
  5. Events / discounts

THE DESIGN

To start the process, we created 5 rapidly sketched designs and got feedback on what worked and what didn’t.
